REU Site: DE-STRESS

DEsigning Sedimentation & Tectonics Research Experiences for Student Success

A year-long geoscience research experience tailored for students with non-traditional schedules

Program Objectives:

  • Provide students with real-world research and training experiences
  • Familiarize students with field and laboratory techniques
  • Highlight geoscience research importance for urban/suburban areas
  • Broaden student perspectives on geoscience careers and educational paths

These objectives are accomplished through mentored team projects incorporating field, lab, and professional development components while achieving a common research goal of improving understanding of the long- and short-term slip history and evolution of the San Andreas Fault system.

Program Overview

A unique year-long program designed for students who may be unable to participate in traditional 6-8 week summer REU programs due to work, family, or financial obligations.

Year-Long Format

Two intensive 2-week sessions (winter field work at CSUSB, summer lab work at UC) plus virtual meetings and workshops throughout the academic year.

San Andreas Fault Research

Study sediments and geomorphic surfaces that record the tectonic evolution of the San Andreas fault system near San Bernardino, California.

Cohort-Based Learning

Work as part of a research team with fellow students and faculty mentors from CSUSB and University of Cincinnati.

Conference Presentation

Present your research at the Geological Society of America national meeting with full travel funding provided.

Geology Majors

  • 8-10 slots available for the year-long experience
  • Travel expenses and 5 weeks of stipend ($700/wk) are provided

Community College Students

  • 8-10 slots available for 2-week-long experiences
  • Students local to inland southern CA can participate in field work in January
  • Students local to Cincinnati, OH metro can participate lab work in June
  • Travel expenses and weekly stipend ($700/wk) are provided

Research Focus

Investigating the slip history and evolution of the southern San Andreas fault system through sedimentological, geochronological, and geomorphic analyses.

Sedimentology Track

Analyze middle Miocene to Pleistocene sedimentary basins and deposits. Study facies patterns, clast counts, and provenance to understand basin evolution and fault slip history.

Neotectonics/Geomorphology Track

Use drone-based LiDAR and photogrammetry to map Quaternary surfaces and their relationship to faults. Calculate slip rates from offset geomorphic features.

Geochronology Methods

Apply detrital zircon U-Pb dating, 40Ar/39Ar dating, and Quaternary dating methods (TCN, OSL) to constrain timing of deposition and deformation.

Key Research Questions

  • How do individual faults organize in space and time?
  • How do long-term slip rates compare to recent rates?
  • What drives strain and landscape evolution?

Leads the REU program; studies patterns and drivers of deformation and landscape evolution using structural analysis, sedimentological records, and geo-/thermochronology. Mentors students in structural geology and geochronology.

Structural Geology, Sedimentology, & Thermochronology
